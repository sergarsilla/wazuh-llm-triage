EXAMPLE CORP OPERATIONAL BASELINE (SIMULATED)

Administrator accounts
- On the application hosts (e.g. app-host-01, app-host-02) the default system
  account "ubuntu" is the standard administrator. It routinely runs container
  and service-management commands: docker, docker compose, systemctl, apt,
  journalctl, grep, tail. Low-score anomalies involving these commands by the
  "ubuntu" account are expected operational noise and are almost always false
  positives.

Routine maintenance
- Package updates (apt update / apt upgrade) and container restarts during
  business hours are normal administrative activity.

Genuinely suspicious behaviour (NOT part of the baseline)
- Downloading a script or binary from the Internet and piping it straight into a
  shell (e.g. "curl ... | bash", "wget ... -O- | sh") is never routine
  administration and must be treated as a real threat.
- Spawning a reverse shell (e.g. "nc -e /bin/sh"), base64-decoding a payload
  into an interpreter, or disabling security tooling indicates compromise
  regardless of which account runs it.
- Service accounts (e.g. www-data) running interactive shells or administrative
  commands is abnormal and suspicious.
